Advantages of Seeking Assistance

  • Improved Well-being and Quality of Life
  • Professional Support
  • Customized Treatment Plans
  • Holistic Approach
  • Peer Support and Group Therapy

Improved Well-being and Quality of Life

As you progress in your recovery journey, you can expect improvements in various aspects of your life. These may include better physical health, enhanced mental well-being, improved relationships, increased productivity, and the ability to pursue meaningful goals and aspirations.

Professional Support

Receive comprehensive support and guidance from experienced healthcare professionals specializing in substance use disorders. They will assess your unique situation and provide expert advice on the most effective treatment options available.

Customized Treatment Plans

Access personalized treatment plans tailored to your specific needs and circumstances. These plans may include a combination of counseling, therapy, medication-assisted treatment, and other evidence-based approaches to address your individual challenges and goals.

Holistic Approach

Experience a holistic approach to treatment that addresses not only the physical aspects of substance use but also the underlying psychological, emotional, and social factors contributing to your addiction. This comprehensive approach aims to heal the whole person and promote lasting recovery.

Peer Support and Group Therapy

Engage in group therapy sessions and support groups where you can connect with others who are going through similar experiences. Sharing your challenges, triumphs, and insights with peers can provide valuable encouragement, understanding, and a sense of belonging.

You Have Questions, We Have Answers

If you or your family have questions about help with alcohol and drug problems, phone 867-456-3838 or toll free in Yukon 1-866-456-3838.
For after hours support, phone 867-667-8473.

  • How can I access counselling services for adults?

    • Drop-in counselling available every Wednesday in Whitehorse from 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. Call in the morning for a same-day appointment

    • 1-on-1 counselling sessions tailored to your needs, including the option for just 2 sessions if preferred

    • Recovery support group in Whitehorse every Wednesday from 2:30 p.m. to 4 p.m

    • Screening and assessment for problematic substance use

    • Education and support groups

    • Assistance with preparation for the intensive treatment program

    • Post-treatment support for ongoing recovery

    • Treatment of co-occurring disorders

  • What is available in the intensive treatment program for adults?

    • Free, live-in intensive treatment program for adults

    • Explore the effects of addiction on physical, emotional, psychological, and spiritual well-being

    • Learn essential skills for living a substance-free life

    • Program duration: Minimum 42 days to maximum 90 days

    • Length of stay based on individual goals and recommendations from counselors

  • Is there withdrawal support specifically for youth and adults?

    • Safe and supportive live-in detox program for individuals in Whitehorse and surrounding communities

    • Available for both youth and adults

    • 24-hour care provided

    • Open every day of the year

    • Capacity for 14 adults and 4 youth in a separate unit

  • What kind of help is available for youth?

    We provide addiction prevention, counselling, and treatment services to students in Grades 5 to 12 in Whitehorse schools. Our services include:

    • Individual counselling and support for youth, parents, and families

    • Support with safely withdrawing and recovering from substance use or dependence (see the information about withdrawal support for youth and adults)

    • Screening and assessment for "problematic substance use". This is when your use of drugs or alcohol has negative effects on your health and life

    • Referrals to appropriate resources

    • School-based prevention and counselling

    • Educational workshops for teachers, parents, and students

    • Collaborative counselling and education for youth treatment camps and wilderness activities

  • How can I get coverage for medications?

    • Medications, such as Naltrexone and Acamprosate, can assist individuals with alcohol use disorder in reducing or stopping drinking
    • The Yukon government provides coverage for these Health Canada-approved medications through the Chronic Disease and Disability Benefits Program and Pharmacare
    • Naltrexone is recommended for patients aiming for abstinence or reduced alcohol consumption, with an approval period of 1 year (initial 30 days supply)
    • Acamprosate is used for maintaining abstinence after at least 4 days of alcohol abstinence or for patients with contraindications to Naltrexone, with an approval period of 1 year (initial 30 days supply)
    • Healthcare providers must apply to the Chronic Disease and Disability Benefits Program on behalf of the individual
    • For inquiries, please contact the Chronic Disease and Disability Benefits program at 867-667-5092 (toll-free in the Yukon at 1-800-661-0408, extension 5092)
